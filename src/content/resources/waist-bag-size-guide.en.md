Size is the decision buyers most often get wrong, because "waist bag" covers everything from a 4 cm-deep running belt to a 15 cm-deep crossbody. Order the wrong size and the bag doesn't sell — not because it's badly made, but because it doesn't match what the customer actually needs to carry.

This guide compares the real dimensions across our production range, so you can match size to buyer before you sample.

## Reading the numbers

**Depth is what matters most.** Length and height tell you the footprint, but depth determines whether the bag holds a wallet and power bank or just a phone. A 1 cm depth is a slim belt; 4 cm is minimal carry; 8–10 cm is genuine everyday capacity.

**Weight signals the use case.** Under 0.10 kg means the bag is designed to disappear while you move — running, gym, cycling. Above 0.10 kg usually means more structure, more pockets, more capacity.

**Length relates to fit, not capacity, on belt styles.** A 43 cm "sports running belt" isn't larger than a 36 cm bag — the extra length is the elastic band that wraps the body. Check the adjustable range instead: our belts typically fit **65–115 cm (25.6–45.3 in)** waists.

## Matching size to your buyer

**Fitness and running audiences** → slim belts, 1–4 cm depth. The priority is that the bag doesn't bounce or chafe. Capacity beyond a phone and keys is actively unwanted.

**Everyday lifestyle and streetwear** → classic fanny pack proportions, roughly 34 × 15 × 9 cm. Big enough to be useful, recognizable enough to be on-trend, and the flat front panel gives you the best branding surface.

**Travel, commuter and security-conscious buyers** → larger crossbody styles, 36–37 cm long with 7–10 cm depth. These wear three ways and hold real contents, and the rear anti-theft pocket matters to this group.

**Promotional and event buyers** → lean toward lighter, simpler styles. They ship cheaper, get used more, and the lower cost per unit lets you cover a bigger audience.

## Custom sizing

Every size above is a production style we already run, which means faster sampling and lower minimums. But if your product needs a different footprint — a deeper pocket for a specific device, a longer strap range, a taller profile — custom dimensions are available.

Custom sizing means a new pattern, so expect a slightly longer sampling cycle and a higher minimum than an existing style. Send the dimensions or a reference sample and we'll tell you what's feasible.

## Before you commit

Two things to confirm in writing on any size:

1. **Internal vs external dimensions.** Published specs are external. If your product must fit a specific item, tell us what it is and we'll verify the internal fit at sample stage.
2. **Strap adjustable range.** Especially important for international markets, where average waist sizes differ. Confirm the range rather than assuming.

## FAQ

**What is a standard waist bag size?**
There's no single standard. Classic fanny packs are typically around 34 × 15 × 9 cm, slim running belts run 26–43 cm long with just 1–4 cm depth, and larger crossbody styles reach 36–37 cm long with 7–10 cm depth. The right size depends on what your customer carries.

**How do I know if a waist bag will fit a phone?**
Nearly all of our styles fit modern smartphones, but slim running belts are specifically designed around phone dimensions — some fit up to 6.5". For larger devices or specific models, tell us the model and we'll confirm at sample stage.

**What waist sizes do the straps fit?**
Adjustable straps typically fit 65–115 cm (25.6–45.3 in). If you're selling into a market with different sizing needs, we can adjust the strap range.

**Can I get a custom size?**
Yes. Custom dimensions require a new pattern, which means a slightly longer sampling cycle and a higher minimum than an existing style. Send your dimensions or a reference sample for feasibility.

**Which size is best for promotional giveaways?**
Lighter, simpler styles usually work best — they cost less per unit, ship cheaper, and get used more often, which is the point of a promotional product.
