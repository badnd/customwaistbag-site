There's no single best way to brand a waist bag. There's the right method for your artwork, your fabric and your quantity — and matching those three is what separates a bag that looks like your brand from a stock bag with a logo stuck on it.

This page covers every customization method we run in-house, what each is good for, and where it works best.

## Logo and branding methods

### Screen printing
Ink applied directly to a flat panel. The everyday workhorse.
- **Best for:** bold logos in one to three solid colors
- **Works on:** most fabrics, including Lycra belts and Oxford panels
- **Strengths:** cost-effective at volume, crisp edges, low minimum
- **Limits:** not suited to gradients, photographic images or very fine detail

### Heat transfer
Artwork transferred onto the surface under heat and pressure.
- **Best for:** full-color designs, gradients, detailed artwork
- **Works on:** most synthetic fabrics
- **Strengths:** carries color and detail screen printing can't hold
- **Limits:** slightly different surface feel than a printed-in logo

### Embroidery
Your logo stitched into the fabric.
- **Best for:** premium, textured brand marks
- **Works on:** structured panels — Oxford, nylon, canvas
- **Strengths:** instantly reads as quality; extremely durable
- **Limits:** not suited to thin stretch fabrics like Lycra running belts; fine detail and small text have practical limits

### Woven labels
A separately woven label stitched onto the bag.
- **Best for:** brand tags, consistent private-label identity across reorders
- **Works on:** any style
- **Strengths:** professional, durable, repeatable
- **Limits:** produced separately, so carries its own minimum

### Rubber / PVC patches
A moulded three-dimensional patch.
- **Best for:** sport, outdoor and tactical aesthetics
- **Works on:** structured panels
- **Strengths:** visually strong, weather-resistant, distinctive feel
- **Limits:** requires a mould, so minimums are higher than printing

### Leather patches
A stitched or heat-pressed leather (or PU) patch.
- **Best for:** heritage, lifestyle and premium positioning
- **Works on:** structured styles
- **Strengths:** warm, tactile, high perceived value
- **Limits:** less suited to technical sport styles

### Metal badges
A metal logo plate fixed to the bag.
- **Best for:** a hardware-level premium signal
- **Works on:** structured panels and straps
- **Strengths:** the most "finished product" of all branding options
- **Limits:** tooling required; higher minimum

### Custom zipper pulls
Your logo on the zipper pull itself.
- **Best for:** the detail that makes a bag feel genuinely designed
- **Works on:** all zippered styles
- **Strengths:** subtle, premium, noticed by buyers who care
- **Limits:** custom tooling means a higher minimum

### Reflective print
A print that returns light in low conditions.
- **Best for:** running and night-visibility products
- **Works on:** running belts and sport styles
- **Strengths:** functional and on-brand at once for fitness products
- **Limits:** design must suit a single reflective tone

### Pantone custom dyeing
The bag body dyed to your exact Pantone color.
- **Best for:** brands where color *is* the identity
- **Works on:** most fabrics, subject to dye compatibility
- **Strengths:** nothing else delivers precise on-brand color
- **Limits:** a dedicated dye run, so a higher minimum than stock colors

## Beyond the logo

**Custom colorways.** Choose from stock colors or match a Pantone exactly. Multi-color combinations (contrast zippers, contrast webbing, contrast pulls) are a cost-effective way to look custom without a full dye run.

**Hardware.** Buckles, hooks, zipper sliders and webbing can be specified in different colors and finishes.

**Labels and tags.** Care labels, wash labels, hangtags and inside labels — the details due-diligence retailers check.

**Packaging.** From poly bag to branded hangtag, custom box or retail-ready presentation.

## Choosing a combination that fits your budget

A practical way to layer customization as you grow:

- **Trial run:** stock fabric, stock or near-stock color, screen-printed logo. Prove the product first.
- **Second order:** add a woven label and a contrast detail. The bag starts to feel like yours.
- **Scaling:** custom zipper pulls, patches, Pantone-matched dyeing, custom packaging. Now it's unmistakably your product.

Each added element carries its own setup and minimum, so building up in stages keeps early orders viable while the product proves itself.

## FAQ

**What's the best way to put a logo on a waist bag?**
It depends on artwork and fabric. Screen printing suits bold, simple logos and works on almost anything. Heat transfer handles full-color detail. Embroidery gives a premium feel on structured fabrics but isn't suited to stretch belts. Woven labels and patches offer durable, professional branding.

**Can I use embroidery on a running belt?**
Generally no. Thin stretch fabrics like Lycra don't support embroidery well — the stitching distorts the stretch and can affect comfort. Screen print, heat transfer, reflective print or a woven label are better choices for slim belts.

**Can you match my exact brand color?**
Yes, through Pantone-based custom dyeing. It requires a dedicated dye run, so the minimum is higher than a stock color, but it delivers precise on-brand color that stock swatches can't.

**What's the cheapest way to customize?**
Screen printing on a stock-color bag is the most cost-effective route, and works at the lowest minimums. Contrast hardware (zippers, webbing, pulls) is another low-cost way to look custom.

**Can I combine multiple methods on one bag?**
Yes — a common combination is a printed or embroidered front logo, a woven inside label, and a custom zipper pull. Each element carries its own setup, so combinations affect both cost and minimum.

**Do you offer private label?**
Yes. Private label with your own labels, tags and packaging is standard for OEM/ODM orders. Tell us how you want the bag to arrive and we'll build the spec around it.
